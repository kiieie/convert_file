1. What is a HEIC File?

HEIC (High Efficiency Image Container) is Apple's version of the HEIF (High Efficiency Image File Format). Introduced in iOS 11, it allows iPhones to capture high-quality photos at almost half the file size of traditional JPEGs (JPGs).

However, despite its efficiency, HEIC is still not universally compatible. Many platforms, websites, and older operating systems (like Windows 10 without premium extensions) cannot open it natively. Thus, converting it to JPG remains a common necessity.

2. The Risks of Downloading Free Translators

A quick search online reveals many downloadable programs claiming to convert HEIC files. Be cautious before installing them, as they pose several risks:

  • Adware and Malware: Many free tools bundle unwanted programs or adware during installation.
  • Server Data Collection: Some standard online converters send your images to remote servers for processing. For personal documents or sensitive photos, this is a major privacy risk.
  • Restrictions: They often restrict how many files you can convert for free, forcing you to pay for batch processing.

4. How to Make Your iPhone Save Photos as JPG Directly

If you prefer to capture photos in JPG format directly and avoid converting them later, you can adjust your iPhone's camera settings:

  1. Open the Settings app on your iPhone.
  2. Scroll down and tap Camera.
  3. Tap Formats.
  4. Change it from 'High Efficiency' to 'Most Compatible'.

※ Note: Choosing 'Most Compatible' will save photos in JPG, but they will take up about 1.5x to 2x more storage space on your device.
