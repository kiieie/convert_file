1. Why Merge Images into a PDF?

Converting a stack of images into a single PDF provides several practical benefits:

  • Preserved Layouts: The PDF standard ensures that pages display identically across all devices—including smartphones, Windows PCs, and MacBooks.
  • Easy Document Management: Managing one single file prevents any pages from getting misplaced or sent out of order.
  • Standard Printing Sizes: PDFs align image scales to standard dimensions like A4 or US Letter, making physical printing seamless.

2. The Danger of Server-Based Free Web Converters

Most free online converters send your documents to their remote servers for processing. If you are uploading invoices, legal IDs, or banking details, **sending them to unknown cloud servers poses a high security risk**.

4. How to Take Clean Document Photos

To make your merged PDF look like it was created by a physical document scanner, try these photo-taking tips:

  • Shoot Straight Down: Capture photos directly from above to avoid geometric perspective distortion.
  • Avoid Shadows: Ensure light sources don't cast shadows of your hand or phone onto the paper.
  • Crop Edges First: Use 2Convert's [Crop Tool](/tools/crop.html) to trim away table surfaces or background clutter before merging.
